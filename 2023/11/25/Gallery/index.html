  Etching: Using metal as a master, precision technology is used. From the perspective of plate making technology, copper engraving mainly uses "etching" technology. The artist first coats the polished metal plate with a preservative (such as asphalt) and then draws the image with a pen or knife. Where pens and knives scratch, the preservative will be scraped off, so when acid (usually nitric acid) is applied to the copper plate, the acid will corrode the parts that are not protected by the preservative, creating traces of recessed metal plates. . The corrosive action of acid eliminates the need for artists to engrave metal plates, making copper engravings ideal for producing reproductions of oil paintings. From the perspective of printing principles, copper engravings belong to gravure printing. During printing, the artist fills ink into these notches, erases the ink on the plane outside the notches, and then uses the high pressure of the printing press to transfer the ink to the appropriate paper. When you touch the surface of a gravure-printed painting, you will feel that the ink is raised. The RMB we use every day uses this technology.
